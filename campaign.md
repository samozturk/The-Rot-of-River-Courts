# The Rot of River Courts
The city of Mivon where our story unfolds.

Mivon sits folded into Sellen river like it grew out of the water itself—bridges, barges, and stone buildings stacked in uneven harmony, always humming with trade, prayer, and quiet ambition. Beneath its polite surface, the city never fully sleeps; it simply changes rhythms as the river pulls it forward.

The church is modest compared to the grand temples of other cities—warm stone, river-worn wood, and stained glass that scatters soft color across the pews even on dull days. This morning, its doors are open early: choir practice is about to begin.

**Monika Arkena** is already in place among the choir, standing where she always does, waiting for the first note to set the room in motion. In the same space, **Rihannon Nightbloom** has just finished her daily training and is helping with the children’s lessons, still carrying that post-exertion focus in her posture.

The choir is led by **Sister Elowen Vireth**—a middle-aged priestess with a calm voice that somehow still carries over every distraction in the room.

The tension in the church settles into something quieter by the end of the hymn.

Sister Elowen finally exhales and places a steady hand on Tomas’s shoulder.

*“You are not returning home alone tonight.”*

He gives a weak nod.

Two older choir members guide him gently toward one of the side chambers where the clergy keep cots for the sick and exhausted. 
<br>
The strange heaviness in the room begins to thin once he leaves. Sister Elowen turns back toward Monika and Rihannon. Colored light from the stained glass cuts across the chapel floor between them.

“Whatever this is, we will not solve it standing in a sanctuary pretending not to see it.”

_She walks toward a side table cluttered with parchment maps, wax seals, and prayer records._

Then she points to several marked places across Mivon.

She taps the first location with one finger:

**“The eastern docks.** Three of the afflicted spent time there within the last week. Tomas included. People hear strange things near the water after sunset. Singing, mostly. Most dismiss it after daylight comes.

Her finger moves toward another mark deeper in the city.

"**The Velvet Net Tavern.**"

_A faint sigh._

“Riverfolk talk more freely when drunk than when questioned formally. You’ll hear rumors there long before you hear truth from officials.”

Then another.

“**The Archive of Saint Kallus.** “If this has happened before—and I suspect it has—records may exist there.”
_Her voice lowers slightly._

“Brother Cassian dislikes visitors. Endure him anyway.”

<br></br>

She slides the final marker beneath part of the river district.

A place where old water tunnels vein beneath the city.

“**The abandoned floodgate.**”

_This time there is small hesitation but visible._


“Children claim they hear voices there.Smugglers avoid it. And two watchmen refused patrol reassignment after a night shift nearby.”

Silence hangs for a moment before she finally leans back from the table.

“You are not city investigators. Nor priests formally tasked with this burden. Which means you still possess something the rest of us are beginning to lose.”

_Her eyes briefly move between Monika and Rihannon._

“Perspective.”

Then after a pause:

“One more thing.”

She reaches for a sealed letter resting near the map. The wax bears the insignia of a weeping lily.
